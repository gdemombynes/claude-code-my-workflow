## Main Argument
Technology adoption in developing countries is constrained by a combination of demand-side factors (firm capabilities, human capital) and supply-side factors (infrastructure, institutional quality). The gap between frontier technology and actual adoption in LMICs is large and persistent.

## Key Findings (likely)
- Technology adoption follows a hierarchy: general-purpose technologies → sector-specific technologies → frontier technologies
- Most firms in LMICs are far from the technology frontier
- Enabling factors (skills, infrastructure, institutions) determine which technologies can be profitably adopted
- AI/digital technologies face the same adoption constraints as earlier technology waves, amplified

## Relevance to Paper
- Provides theoretical grounding for the enabling factors framework
- Historical precedent: OLPC, e-learning, telemedicine all faced similar adoption barriers
- Justifies skepticism about AI deployment at scale in LMICs without addressing enabling factors
