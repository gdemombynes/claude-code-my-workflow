# WHO/World Bank/IRENA/SEforALL (2023) — Electrification of Health Care Facilities

**Full citation:** World Health Organization, World Bank, International Renewable Energy Agency, & Sustainable Energy for All. (2023). *Tracking SDG 7: The Energy Progress Report.* Washington, DC: World Bank.
**⚠️ Verify:** The specific report title may differ. The data on health facility electrification is from WHO/World Bank joint tracking of SDG 7. Search "WHO World Bank electrification health facilities 2023" to confirm exact citation.
**Type:** Data report / international statistics
**Coverage:** Global; with sub-national data for some regions

## Key Data Points (as cited in draft)
- 86% of hospitals in Latin America and the Caribbean have reliable electricity access
- 50% of hospitals in Sub-Saharan Africa have reliable electricity access
- Rates for non-hospital facilities (clinics, health posts) and rural facilities are lower

## Relevance to Paper
- Primary data source for Figure 1 (electricity access) or enabling factors section
- Establishes the baseline infrastructure gap that limits AI deployment in health facilities
- The 50% figure for SSA hospitals is for hospitals — primary care and rural facilities are significantly lower

## Limitations / Caveats
- Data are fragmentary — many LMICs have poor facility-level reporting
- "Reliable access" definition may vary across countries
- Hospital figures mask lower rates at primary care level
