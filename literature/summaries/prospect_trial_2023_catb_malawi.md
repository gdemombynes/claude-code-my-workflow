# PROSPECT Trial (2023/2024) — CAD4TB Screening, Malawi

**Full citation:** ⚠️ VERIFY FULL CITATION. The PROSPECT trial in Malawi on AI-assisted TB screening was published in PLOS Medicine or PLOS ONE, approximately 2023–2024. Search "PROSPECT trial CAD4TB Malawi tuberculosis AI screening" to confirm authors and exact citation.
**Type:** Three-arm RCT
**Country/Region:** Malawi
**Sample:** Adults with TB symptoms

## Main Research Question
Does AI-assisted chest X-ray screening (CAD4TB) improve TB case detection and treatment initiation compared to standard care?

## Data & Methods
Three-arm randomized trial testing CAD4TB (an AI tool for reading chest X-rays) for adults presenting with TB symptoms, alongside routine HIV testing.

## Key Findings
- 3.0% of patients in AI screening arm started TB treatment within 56 days vs. 1.1% under standard care
- Median time to treatment: dropped from 11 days to 1 day
- Increased same-day TB treatment; reduced fraction of undiagnosed TB and HIV cases
- Cost-effectiveness was mixed: life-saving but not cost-effective over short (2-month) horizon

## Relevance to Paper
- One of the few RCTs of AI for health in an LMIC
- Strong causal evidence that AI imaging tool can improve detection and treatment speed for a major LMIC disease burden (TB)
- Cost-effectiveness caveat is important for policy audience: even effective AI may not be cost-effective at scale without longer time horizons
- Example of **direct provision** modality (AI reads X-ray without specialist)

## Limitations / Caveats
- Short 2-month follow-up window limits cost-effectiveness analysis
- Scalability requires radiology infrastructure; X-ray machines not universally available
- Longer-term outcomes (mortality, disease progression) not measured
