# De Simone et al. (2025) — Microsoft Copilot English Tutor, Nigeria

**Full citation:** De Simone, G., et al. (2025). *AI-Powered English Tutoring in Nigeria: Experimental Evidence.* World Bank Policy Research Working Paper.
**⚠️ Verify:** Exact title and working paper number. Search "De Simone World Bank Nigeria Copilot 2025". First author may be Gianluca De Simone.
**Type:** RCT (small-scale pilot)
**Country/Region:** Edo State, Nigeria
**Sample:** First-year secondary school students; small-scale pilot
**Time period:** Six-week after-school program

## Main Research Question
Can a guided after-school AI tutoring program (using Microsoft Copilot/GPT-4) improve English language and digital skills among secondary students in Nigeria?

## Data & Methods
Randomized control trial. Treatment: six-week after-school program using Microsoft Copilot (GPT-4) as a guided English tutor, supervised by teachers. All schools selected had electricity. Internet access provided through mobile routers. Laptops provided from World Bank surplus equipment. Teachers received training.

## Key Findings
- 0.23 SD gains in English (intent-to-treat)
- 0.31 SD gains on composite (English + AI/digital skills)
- All four enabling factors had to be provided externally: electricity, internet, devices, teacher training

## Relevance to Paper
- One of only 3 published causal studies of GenAI in education in LMICs
- Critical illustration of enabling factors: intervention required external provision of electricity, internet, devices, and training — none normally available at typical Nigerian public schools
- Only 47% of public junior secondary schools in Nigeria have electricity; 31% have computers
- Shows scalability is the central challenge: the enabling factors that made the RCT work are not present at scale

## Limitations / Caveats
- Very small pilot; limited statistical power
- All enabling factors externally provided — not representative of real-world conditions
- Short duration (6 weeks) — no evidence on persistence
- Preprint / working paper status: check for peer review update
