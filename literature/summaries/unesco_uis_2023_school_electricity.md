# UNESCO Institute for Statistics (2023) — School Electricity Access

**Full citation:** UNESCO Institute for Statistics. (2023). *Education Data.* UIS Data Browser. Retrieved from http://uis.unesco.org.
**⚠️ Verify:** UIS data is available at uis.unesco.org — confirm the specific indicator used and most recent year of data. The figures in the draft are labeled as "2023 data."
**Type:** Administrative data / international statistics
**Coverage:** Global; country-level

## Key Data Points (as cited in draft)
Primary schools with electricity access:
- Chad: 4%
- Democratic Republic of Congo: 10%
- Madagascar: 11%

Lower secondary schools:
- Chad: 23%
- DRC: 12%
- Madagascar: 49%

## Relevance to Paper
- Primary data source for Figure 1 (school electricity access) or enabling factors section
- Illustrates extreme baseline infrastructure constraints for AI in education in the lowest-income countries
- The jump from primary to lower secondary (e.g., Madagascar 11% → 49%) is itself a notable finding

## Notes on Use
- UIS data is based on country reporting and may have quality issues in some countries
- These three countries represent near-worst-case scenarios; figures are better in other LMICs (e.g., India, Kenya)
- Important to contextualize: these are the countries where AI access is hardest, not representative of all LMICs
