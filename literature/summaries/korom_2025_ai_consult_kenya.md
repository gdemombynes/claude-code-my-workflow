# Korom et al. (2025) — AI Consult Clinical Decision Support, Kenya

**Full citation:** Korom, R., et al. (2025). *AI Clinical Decision Support in Primary Care: Evidence from Kenya.* Preprint.
**⚠️ Verify:** Exact title, authors, preprint server. Search "AI Consult Penda Health Kenya Korom 2025".
**Type:** Quasi-experimental (comparison between clinicians with/without AI Consult access)
**Country/Region:** Nairobi, Kenya (Penda Health network)
**Sample:** 15 primary care clinics; physicians with and without access to AI Consult
**Time period:** Not specified (recent)

## Main Research Question
Does an AI clinical support tool integrated into electronic workflows improve the quality of care at primary care clinics in a LMIC?

## Data & Methods
Comparison study: physicians with access to AI Consult vs. those without. AI Consult is integrated with electronic workflows and flags potential documentation and clinical errors in real-time. Errors were identified by independent physician evaluators reviewing clinical decisions.

## Key Findings
- 16% fewer diagnostic errors among those using AI Consult
- 13% fewer treatment errors among those using AI Consult
- All surveyed users said AI Consult improved quality of their care

## Relevance to Paper
- First solid evidence that clinical decision support can improve quality of care in an LMIC setting
- Example of **augmentation modality** — AI assists physician, human remains in the loop
- Critical enabling factor context: Penda Health is a for-profit social enterprise with strong administrative structure, full EMR adoption since 2017, and pre-existing rule-based guidance — far above average for LMICs
- Raises generalizability question: most LMIC public primary care facilities lack EMR, making this specific AI tool impossible to deploy

## Limitations / Caveats
- Not a randomized design — selection into AI use may bias results
- Error evaluation disagreement among independent physician reviewers — measuring quality of care is inherently difficult
- Penda Health is unusually well-organized for an LMIC facility; findings may not generalize to public sector
- Preprint: not peer-reviewed
