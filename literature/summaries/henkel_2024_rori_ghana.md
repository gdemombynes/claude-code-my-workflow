# Henkel et al. (2024) — Rori AI Tutor, Ghana

**Full citation:** Henkel, L., et al. (2024). *Can AI Tutoring Improve Learning in Low-Income Countries? Evidence from Ghana.* Preprint.
**⚠️ Verify:** Exact title, preprint server (likely SSRN or arXiv or CGD working paper), and full author list. Search "Rori AI tutor Ghana Henkel 2024".
**Type:** RCT
**Country/Region:** Ghana
**Sample:** ~1,000 students, grades 3–9, 11 schools (Rising Academies network)
**Time period:** 2023–2024 school year (8 months)

## Main Research Question
Can a WhatsApp-based AI math tutor improve student learning outcomes in a low-income country context?

## Data & Methods
Randomized control trial. Treatment: students received two 30-minute Rori tutoring sessions per week (via WhatsApp on Android phones on 2G/3G networks) in addition to regular math classes. Control: regular classes only. Outcome: math test scores.

## Key Findings
- Effect size: ~0.36 SD improvement in math scores after one school year
- Delivered via minimal technology — basic Android phones on 2G/3G networks
- WhatsApp delivery reduces infrastructure requirements compared to app-based approaches

## Relevance to Paper
- One of only 3 published causal studies of GenAI in education in LMICs
- Strongest effect size among the LMIC GenAI education studies
- Important caveat: conducted in Rising Academies, a low-cost private school network with stronger institutional structure than typical public schools
- Demonstrates that AI education tools can work with minimal technology (enabling factors lesson)

## Limitations / Caveats
- Rising Academies provides institutional support (trained staff, monitoring) not available in most public schools — limits generalizability
- Preprint: not yet peer-reviewed
- Short-term outcomes only; no follow-up on persistence of gains
- Math only — no evidence on other subjects
