# ChatGPT / GenAI in Education — Reviews Group Summary

**Papers in this group (all ⚠️ need full citation verification):**
- Wang & Fan (2025) — meta-analysis of ChatGPT on learning performance; 51 studies; 0.867 SD effect size
- Deng et al. (2024) — systematic review of ChatGPT in education
- Munaye et al. (2025) — systematic review of ChatGPT and student learning

**Type:** Systematic reviews / meta-analyses
**Coverage:** Predominantly high-income country settings; some middle-income

## Shared Research Question
What is the overall effect of ChatGPT / generative AI tools on student learning outcomes?

## Key Findings
- Wang & Fan (2025): pooled effect size of 0.867 SD across 51 studies — large by education standards
- Effects vary widely across studies; publication bias likely inflates pooled estimates
- Most studies are short-term, small-scale, and conducted in university settings in high-income countries
- Little to no coverage of primary/secondary education in LMICs in these reviews

## Relevance to Paper
- Establishes baseline optimism from the general education-AI literature
- Critical contrast with the 3 LMIC-specific RCTs (Choi, Henkel, De Simone), which show smaller and more context-dependent effects
- Useful framing device: 0.867 SD is what proponents cite; the LMIC evidence is much thinner
- Underscores selection bias in the review literature: studies that work get published; context-specific failures don't

## Limitations / Caveats
- Severe publication bias — most positive results
- Mostly university-age students in high-income settings; not applicable to primary school in SSA
- Short-term outcomes; no evidence on persistence
- Most studies lack proper control groups (comparison is often "no AI" vs "AI" without randomization)
- Large heterogeneity in what counts as "ChatGPT use"
