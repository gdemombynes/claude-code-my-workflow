# Choi et al. (2024) — Teacher.AI in Sierra Leone

**Full citation:** Choi, J., et al. (2024). *How Do Teachers in a Low-Resource Setting Use an AI Teaching Assistant?* Preprint.
**⚠️ Verify:** Exact journal/preprint server and full author list need confirmation. Search "Teacher.AI Sierra Leone Choi 2024".
**Type:** Descriptive (not causal)
**Country/Region:** Sierra Leone
**Sample:** Teachers at unspecified number of schools; 17 months of usage data
**Time period:** ~2022–2024

## Main Research Question
How do teachers in a low-resource setting actually use an AI tool, and what kinds of queries do they make?

## Data & Methods
Descriptive analysis of query logs from Teacher.AI, a WhatsApp-integrated OpenAI assistant prompted as an education assistant. Tool was tailored to provide evidence-based pedagogy guidance, operate within safeguards, answer only education-related questions, and be appropriate for low-resource settings.

## Key Findings
- 69% of queries were for facts and concept clarification — tasks that a non-AI web search could handle
- Main advantage of the AI tool: provides web-search-like information at much lower friction via WhatsApp
- Suggests the primary value of AI in low-resource teacher settings may be convenience, not novel capability

## Relevance to Paper
- Example of the **augmentation modality** (AI assisting teachers, not replacing them)
- Demonstrates that even in low-infrastructure settings (WhatsApp on 2G), teachers can access AI tools
- Raises question: if most queries are factual, is this different from a search engine?
- Relevant to enabling factors section: WhatsApp as delivery mechanism reduces device/internet barriers

## Limitations / Caveats
- Descriptive only — no causal evidence of impact on teaching quality or student outcomes
- Single network, may not generalize
- Selection: teachers who adopted the tool may differ from non-adopters
