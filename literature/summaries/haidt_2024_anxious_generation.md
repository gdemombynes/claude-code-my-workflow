# Haidt (2024) — The Anxious Generation

**Full citation:** Haidt, J. (2024). *The Anxious Generation: How the Great Rewiring of Childhood Is Causing an Epidemic of Mental Illness.* Penguin Press.
**Type:** Trade book / social science synthesis
**Author:** Jonathan Haidt (NYU Stern School of Business)

## Main Argument
The rapid spread of smartphones and social media among adolescents (especially from ~2012 onward) has caused a significant increase in rates of depression, anxiety, and loneliness, particularly among girls. Haidt argues this constitutes a mental health crisis driven by the "great rewiring of childhood."

## Key Findings / Claims
- Sharp rise in adolescent mental health problems coincides with smartphone adoption
- Social media displaces face-to-face interaction, sleep, and other protective activities
- Girls are more affected than boys (social comparison, cyberbullying)
- Haidt advocates for phone-free schools and delaying social media access until age 16

## Relevance to Paper
- Cited in the Perils section as evidence of adverse mental health spillovers from digital technology
- Provides a cautionary framework: previous digital technology waves had unintended negative consequences on youth well-being
- Relevant to the AI perils discussion: even well-intentioned technology can have harmful side effects
- Also relevant for education section: screen time and digital distraction as risks of AI-based learning tools

## Limitations / Caveats
- Trade book, not peer-reviewed; some of Haidt's causal claims are contested by researchers (e.g., Orben, Przybylski)
- Evidence base is primarily from high-income countries; LMIC evidence is thin
- Haidt focuses on social media specifically; extension to AI tools is inferential
- Correlation vs. causation debate is ongoing in academic literature
